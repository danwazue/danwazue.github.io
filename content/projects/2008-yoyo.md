<h2 class="project-body-heading">Introduction</h2>

<p class="project-body">2.008 (Design and Manufacturing II) is MIT's production-scale manufacturing course. Rather than building one-off prototypes, each team designs a yo-yo and then manufactures <strong>50 complete units</strong> using the same industrial processes found in real factories: injection molding, thermoforming, and CNC machining.</p>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Goal:</strong> Design a yo-yo that meets all class constraints, can be manufactured repeatably at quantity, and holds together via a press fit with no glue or fasteners holding the halves together.</p>

<p class="project-body">This page documents the project as it unfolds. We are currently past the Design Review (Week 6) with molds machined and first injection molded parts in hand.</p>

<h2 class="project-body-heading">Parts & Molds</h2>

<p class="project-body">Our yo-yo, nicknamed <strong>"Yubba Yubba"</strong> (a Hubba Bubba-themed design), consists of 12 total parts:</p>

<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>2 injection molded bodies</li>
  <li>2 injection molded rings</li>
  <li>2 inner thermoforms</li>
  <li>2 outer thermoforms</li>
  <li>2 vinyl decals</li>
  <li>1 insert-molded nut (COTS)</li>
  <li>1 shoulder bolt (COTS)</li>
</ul>

<p class="project-body">Manufacturing these required 5 molds:</p>

<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>1 core-cavity mold for the ring</li>
  <li>1 core mold for the body (cavity not machined)</li>
  <li>2 thermoforming molds (inner: 1.842", outer: 1.91")</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="project-body-heading">Design for Manufacturing</h2>

<p class="project-body">Every feature on the yo-yo had to be evaluated for manufacturability. The constraints we designed around:</p>

<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>At least 2 injection molded parts and 1 thermoformed part</li>
  <li>Draft angles on all IM surfaces for clean ejection (2° on both ring and body)</li>
  <li>Uniform wall thickness (~0.08") to prevent sink marks and warping</li>
  <li>All molds must include sprue, ejector pins, gates, and runners</li>
  <li>All features machinable on a 3-axis CNC mill</li>
  <li>Insert-molded nut viable with the body mold geometry</li>
</ul>

<h3 class="project-body-heading">Ring</h3>

<p class="project-body">The ring features uniform wall thickness (~0.08"), 2° draft angle, 1/32" fillet, 0.102" fillet, and the interference fit surface (the critical dimension for press fit).</p>

<h3 class="project-body-heading">Body</h3>

<p class="project-body">The body features uniform wall thickness (~0.08"), 2° draft angle, the mating interference fit surface, and provisions for the insert-molded nut, sprue, ejector pins, and gate/runner.</p>

<h3 class="project-body-heading">Thermoform</h3>

<p class="project-body">Inner thermoform mold at 1.842", outer thermoform mold at 1.91", with a 0.035" fillet. The thermoform carries the Hubba Bubba vinyl decal.</p>

<h2 class="project-body-heading">Press Fit Engineering</h2>

<p class="project-body">The press fit holds the yo-yo together with no fasteners, so getting the interference right was the central engineering problem. Our approach combined a baseline recommendation with analytical validation.</p>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Parameters:</strong></p>

<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>Nominal interference: 0.015"</li>
  <li>Length of engagement: 0.200"</li>
</ul>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Drop test scenario:</strong> A 4 ft fall generates ~250 Gs, producing ~5.1 lbs of escape force on the press fit.</p>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Holding force analysis:</strong></p>

<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>Worst case (0.005" interference): ~12.6 lbs holding force, safety factor ≈ 2.5</li>
  <li>Nominal (0.015" interference): ~37.9 lbs holding force, safety factor ≈ 7.4</li>
</ul>

<p class="project-body">The holding force was calculated using thick-walled cylinder pressure equations and a friction coefficient of 0.2. Impact velocity from the drop test:</p>

<p class="project-body">v = √(2 · g · h) = √(2 · 32.2 · 4) ≈ 16.05 ft/s</p>

<p class="project-body">Escape force from impulse:</p>

<p class="project-body">F = (m · v) / t = (0.02056 × 16.05) / 0.002 ≈ 5.1 lbs</p>

<p class="project-body">Even at the worst-case interference of 0.005", the holding force exceeds the escape force by a factor of 2.5.</p>

<h3 class="project-body-heading">Shrinkage & Scaling</h3>

<p class="project-body">Getting the press fit right required working backwards from the desired interference at room temperature, accounting for the shrinkage of the injected plastic. The mold is scaled by a factor of 1/(1 - %shrinkage) = 1.0204, so parts come out of the mold oversized and shrink to their target dimensions as they cool.</p>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Validation method:</strong></p>

<ol class="project-body-list">
  <li>Injection mold a test part using 2% shrinkage (recommended from previous projects)</li>
  <li>Measure the critical dimension at 5 instances</li>
  <li>Compare the average measured value to the nominal CAD value</li>
  <li>Simulate mold flow in Fusion to identify potential defects (sinks, warping, air traps) and check recommended shrinkage</li>
</ol>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Results:</strong></p>

<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>Outer: Nominal 2.160 ± 0.005 in, adjusted CAD 2.204 in, measured 2.163 in, simulation recommended 1.533% shrinkage</li>
  <li>Inner: Nominal 2.145 ± 0.005 in, adjusted CAD 2.189 in, measured 2.141 in, simulation recommended 1.739% shrinkage</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="project-body-heading">CNC Machining the Molds</h2>

<p class="project-body">Molds were programmed in Fusion 360 and cut on the <strong>HAAS VF2ss VMC</strong> in the LMP machine shop. We used probing to set work offsets precisely before each run.</p>

<h3 class="project-body-heading">Ring Mold</h3>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Core side:</strong></p>
<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>Facing: 1 min 9 sec</li>
  <li>Ejector pins: pilot drill (11 sec) + deep drilling (3 min 7 sec)</li>
  <li>Rough cuts: adaptive (16 min) + contour (13 min 19 sec)</li>
  <li>Finishing cuts: flat (2 min 55 sec) + contour (32 min 6 sec) + bore (2 min 50 sec)</li>
  <li>Press fit surface: finishing scallop (12 min 31 sec)</li>
</ul>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Cavity side:</strong> Facing, rough cuts (adaptive, scallop, flat, contour), finishing cuts (ramp, inner/outer edge pencil, contour), and features for gate, runner, and sprue.</p>

<h3 class="project-body-heading">Body Mold</h3>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Core side:</strong></p>
<ul class="project-body-list">
  <li>Facing: 1 min 9 sec</li>
  <li>Rough cuts: adaptive (3 min 5 sec) + flat (1 min 14 sec) + bore (16 sec)</li>
  <li>Ejector pins: pilot drill (11 sec) + deep drilling (2 min 49 sec)</li>
  <li>Press fit surface: taper bore (3 min 44 sec) + bore (4 min 3 sec)</li>
  <li>Finishing cuts: ramp (7 min 23 sec) + gate contour (5 sec)</li>
  <li>Features for sprue, ejector pins, insert-molded nut, and gate/runner</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="project-body-heading">High Risk Features</h2>

<p class="project-body">After the first test parts, we identified several areas that needed attention:</p>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Surface defects:</strong> Noticeable surface defects from the mold on the IM test part, including a sink mark risk location and features that were difficult to machine cleanly.</p>

<p class="project-body"><strong>CAD/CAM redesign:</strong> We plan to change to a 1/8" radius fillet and straighten edges to reduce CNC time by ~20 min, and adjust the CAM for a smoother finish.</p>

<p class="project-body"><strong>Decal incorporation:</strong> The Hubba Bubba design uses stickers on the outside face of the thermoform. We are evaluating alternatives: printing on the adhesive side of clear vinyl, or using a two-layered thermoform to protect the decal, though there are concerns about layer adhesion and effectiveness.</p>
