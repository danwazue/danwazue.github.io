Hey there! I'm Daniel, a mechanical engineering student at MIT studying Controls, Instrumentation, and Robotics (Course 2A‑6). I'm passionate about designing and building systems that integrate mechanical design, manufacturing, sensing, and intelligent control. I enjoy working through the full engineering process—from concept and CAD to fabrication and testing.

Right now, I'm designing and manufacturing 50 yo-yos for MIT's 2.008 Design and Manufacturing II class, where students develop a product and produce it in quantity using industrial manufacturing methods. My experience includes:

  • Research and educational manufacturing systems at the MIT Learning Engineering and Practice Group (LEAP), assembling a CNC milling machine and co-authoring instructional documentation for future students
  • Vehicle testing and engineering analysis at OX Delivers, working on electric truck testing procedures including system inspections, fault detection, and CAN-based data logging
  • Robotics design and fabrication for MIT's 2.007 Design and Manufacturing I, building a fully functional competition robot through mechanical design, CAD, and hands-on fabrication

Outside of engineering, I enjoy building community on campus. I lead an African/Afro-Caribbean Bible study and am also a member of the Gordon Engineering Leadership (GEL) program at MIT.

Feel free to check out my resume or email me if you'd like to connect!
